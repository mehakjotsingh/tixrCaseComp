3 Key Risks Per Market
Germany
  • CTS Eventim dominates German ticketing — enterprise RFP cycles at large arenas run 6–18 months; prioritize the 200+ mid-market arenas (2,250–5,000 cap, RS 70.7–72.7) where Eventim's sales team is less active and no confirmed exclusives exist1
  • SEPA, Klarna, and Sofort payment integrations are commercial blockers — must be live in checkout before first German event goes on sale
  • Expanded pipeline (611 T1+T2 venues) requires partner-led / self-serve onboarding model — direct enterprise sales alone will not scale; begin partner program framework by Day 45
Netherlands
  • Melkweg and Paradiso locked to Ticketmaster/Paylogic3,4 — approach via promoters booking these venues; do not pitch venue directly
  • ADE 2026 conference contract held by Weeztix5 — ADE brand presence must come through independent festival night promoter deals, not the umbrella organization
  • Pipeline of 820 T1+T2 venues requires self-serve onboarding — the Netherlands Partner Program (self-serve layer) is essential to capturing long-tail pipeline beyond the 10 enterprise targets
  • iDEAL payment integration is a hard requirement — absence materially drops Dutch checkout conversion
Australia
  • Ticketek (TEG) holds exclusive or preferred contracts at Rod Laver, John Cain, Hamer Hall6,7 — and likely additional Melbourne Park venues; all must be approached via independent promoters who book events and own ticketing decisions
  • Crown Theatre Perth locked to Ticketmaster8; Crown Live Theatre relaunch (Nov 2026) is the single most time-sensitive greenfield deal — proposal must be submitted before Aug 2026 or risk incumbent re-sign
  • Geographic distance from Santa Monica HQ requires a regional ops lead in Melbourne or Sydney — hire by Day 20; BPAY/POLi/Afterpay integration required for consumer checkout
